# Image Art References

Use this file when generated deck-art quality or style choice is ambiguous.

## Universal Standard

- Use one complete 16:9 composition for one slide.
- Keep full subjects visible with generous margins.
- Avoid contact-sheet slicing, accidental edge crop, or blank space caused by aspect-ratio mismatch.
- Avoid readable text, logos, and watermarks.
- Inspect generated images before placing them into slides.
- Treat every reference image as a treatment sample, not a scene template. Extract palette, lighting, texture, material, linework, composition, and emotional tone; discard its people, place, activity, objects, and narrative unless the slide explicitly calls for them.
- Choose the subject from the slide claim first. A compelling image makes the idea natural and legible, then applies the selected treatment.

## Available Styles

### Formal Business

Reference: `formal-business-reference.png`

Treatment: polished editorial photography; dark walnut, charcoal, navy, and off-white; subtle gold; precise lighting; formal composition; high-trust materials; measured executive tone. Do not copy the reference desk, documents, or office setting by default.

### Professional HR

Reference: `professional-hr-reference.png`

Treatment: warm, human-centered editorial photography; natural light; wood and soft-gray materials; muted teal, coral, sage, navy, and off-white; inclusive and credible tone. Do not copy the reference workshop, presenter, group, wall display, or meeting setting by default.

### AI and Technology

Reference: `ai-technology-reference.png`

Treatment: credible technical editorial photography; graphite and black-blue palette; subtle cyan light; restrained red-orange risk accents; clean, legible forms; serious non-fantasy finish. Do not copy the reference operations room, dashboard wall, lab, or cluster of screens by default.

### Mature Cartoon

Reference: `mature-cartoon-reference.png`

Treatment: sophisticated adult graphic-novel linework; subtle paper texture; muted teal, warm gray, graphite, and cream; restrained coral accents; expressive, credible, training-friendly tone. Do not copy the reference review room, presenter, group, whiteboard, or workplace meeting by default.

### Painterly Human-AI Cooperation

Reference: `complete-slide-art-reference.jpg`

Use editorial watercolor or paper-collage treatment with an artistic, warm, not-too-sci-fi tone. Do not copy the reference conversation, desk, laptop, or office setting unless the slide is specifically about that interaction.
